Sentiment Indicators (Non-AI)

Fear & Greed Index · AAII Survey · Put/Call Ratio · VIX — How to Read, How to Combine
FINN Knowledge Base · HB P6 §6.4 · First appears W08

FINN framing: Sentiment indicators measure the emotional state of the collective market — not the fundamental value of any security. They are most useful at extremes: when sentiment reaches extreme fear or extreme greed, the contrarian signal becomes statistically meaningful. In the middle ranges, they are noise.

No single indicator. One sentiment indicator is an observation. Two confirming indicators are a hypothesis. Three confirming indicators are a signal worth testing. Confluence across multiple indicators is what earns a sentiment read credibility.

CNN Fear & Greed Index — Composite Sentiment Score
CNN Fear & Greed Index
Source: CNN Business · Updated daily · Range: 0–100
Contrarian Indicator
Reading the Scale
0–24 Extreme Fear Contrarian BUY signal. Markets oversold emotionally. Historically precedes sharp bounces. Look for technical confirmation before acting.
25–44 Fear Risk-off environment. Favor defensive positions. Weak breakouts fail here. Watch for further deterioration or capitulation reversal.
45–55 Neutral No directional sentiment edge. Most reliable for mean-reversion strategies. Neither bulls nor bears have clear sentiment advantage.
56–74 Greed Risk-on. Breakouts tend to follow through. Momentum strategies work. Begin watching for complacency signs as the upper range approaches.
75–100 Extreme Greed Contrarian SELL/CAUTION signal. Markets overbought emotionally. Historically precedes corrections. Tighten stops; reduce new long exposure.
Inputs (7 sub-indicators)
Market Momentum (SPY vs 125-day MA) · Stock Price Strength (52-week highs vs lows) · Stock Price Breadth · Put/Call Ratio · Junk Bond Demand · Market Volatility (VIX) · Safe Haven Demand (stock vs bond returns)
FINN Use Case
Check weekly as regime context. Extreme readings (0–24, 75–100) warrant heightened attention. In Extreme Fear, prioritize setups with strong volume confirmation — when sentiment reverses, moves can be fast.
Key Limitation
Timing is uncertain. Extreme Fear can get more extreme before reversal. It is a condition, not a trigger. Still need price confirmation — a reversal candle, a breakout, a volume signal.
Where to Find
CNN Business website · Fear and Greed section · Historical chart available — use it to calibrate current reading against prior extremes
AAII Sentiment Survey — Retail Investor Pulse
AAII Investor Sentiment Survey
Source: American Association of Individual Investors · Published weekly (Thursday) · Survey of retail investors
Contrarian Indicator
Reading the Numbers
% Bearish > 50% Extreme Pessimism Strong contrarian bullish signal. Historical average bearish reading is ~31%. Above 50% is statistically rare and has preceded market recoveries.
Bullish ≈ 38% Historical Average No directional edge from sentiment alone. Need other indicators for confluence.
% Bullish > 55% Extreme Optimism Contrarian caution signal. When retail investors are overwhelmingly bullish, the market is often near a short-term top. Historical average bullish is ~38%.
Bull-Bear Spread: Bullish% minus Bearish%. Spread below −20 = notable pessimism. Spread above +30 = notable optimism. Track direction of change, not just level.
Why It Works as Contrarian
Retail investors are the "dumb money" in academic literature — statistically, their extreme positions tend to mark turning points. When they all agree, the trade is crowded and vulnerable to reversal.
FINN Use Case
Check weekly on Thursday release. Most valuable at extremes. Combine with VIX and Fear & Greed for confluence. A 3-indicator sentiment confirmation is more actionable than any single reading.
Key Limitation
Survey-based — subject to response bias. Also: retail sentiment can stay extreme longer than expected. This is a regime indicator, not a precise timing tool.
Historical Base Rates
Bullish avg: ~38% · Bearish avg: ~31% · Neutral avg: ~31%. Readings that deviate more than 1 standard deviation from these averages signal notable sentiment extremes.
CBOE Put/Call Ratio — Options Market Fear Gauge
CBOE Total Put/Call Ratio (PCR)
Source: CBOE.com · Updated intraday · Total PCR = (total put volume) / (total call volume)
Contrarian Indicator
Reading the Ratio
PCR < 0.60 Extreme Call Buying Contrarian bearish signal. Market participants buying lots of calls = excessive optimism. Often precedes pullbacks or tops.
0.60–0.79 Call-Leaning Mild optimism in options market. Breakouts more likely to follow through. No extreme signal.
0.80–1.00 Neutral Range Historical average ~0.85–0.90. No directional sentiment edge from PCR alone. Monitor for directional drift.
1.00–1.20 Put-Leaning Elevated hedging or put-buying. Risk-off sentiment building. Not yet at extreme but worth watching.
PCR > 1.20 Extreme Put Buying Contrarian bullish signal. Panic hedging often marks near-term bottoms. Markets frequently reverse up sharply from extreme PCR levels.
Equity PCR vs. Total PCR
Use equity-only PCR (excludes index options) for most precise retail sentiment read. Total PCR includes institutional hedging which distorts the signal. Both available on CBOE.
5-Day / 10-Day Moving Average
Single-day PCR is noisy. Use a 5-day or 10-day MA of the PCR for smoother, more reliable readings. Extreme levels on the MA are more significant than single-day spikes.
FINN Use Case
Monitor the 10-day MA daily during the DMB. When PCR MA moves to extremes, flag as regime context for that week's trade setups. High PCR + Fear & Greed extreme = strong confluence setup.
Key Limitation
Options data includes institutional hedging programs that move the ratio independent of retail sentiment. The equity-only PCR is cleaner but still not perfect isolation of directional positioning.
VIX — Volatility Index (The "Fear Index")
CBOE Volatility Index (VIX)
Source: CBOE.com · Real-time · Measures implied 30-day volatility of S&P 500 options · Expressed as annualized %
Direct + Contrarian
VIX Level Zones
VIX <12
Extreme complacency. Volatility priced very low. Often precedes volatility spikes.
Watch: calm markets can turn fast. Tighten stops on existing longs.
12–18
Normal low-volatility environment. Trend-following strategies perform well. SPY expected daily move: ~0.6–1.1%.
Standard operating conditions. Systematic strategies work as designed.
18–25
Elevated uncertainty. Larger intraday swings. Wider stops needed. SPY expected daily move: ~1.1–1.6%.
Reduce position size by 25–33%. Widen stops proportionally.
25–35
High fear. Market in risk-off mode. SPY expected daily move: ~1.6–2.2%. Whipsaw risk elevated.
Defensive posture. Reduce exposure significantly. No new aggressive longs.
VIX >35
Crisis/panic zone. SPY expected daily move: 2%+. Extreme fear. Historically marks significant market bottoms over 1–3 month horizon.
Contrarian signal: start watching for reversal setups. Wait for stabilization.
VIX as Regime Indicator
VIX tells you the current volatility environment — this determines position sizing, stop width, and expected move magnitude. Your strategy parameters must adapt to the regime.
VIX Contango / Backwardation
When VIX futures curve is in contango (near-term VIX lower than longer-term): low current vol expectation, calm regime. Backwardation (near-term higher): immediate fear, uncertainty front-loaded.
FINN Position Sizing Rule
VIX 12–18: standard size (1× risk)
VIX 18–25: reduced size (0.75× risk)
VIX 25–35: small size (0.5× risk)
VIX >35: minimal or no new positions
VIX vs. VIX9D
VIX = 30-day implied vol. VIX9D = 9-day implied vol. When VIX9D spikes dramatically above VIX → acute near-term fear event, often short-lived. Watch for rapid normalization.

"Sentiment is not the trade — it's the weather report. You still need a technical setup, a defined entry, a stop, and a target. What sentiment tells you is whether the wind is at your back or in your face. Extreme fear with a bullish reversal candle on above-average volume — that is a setup. Extreme fear alone is a condition. What does the price action say?"
