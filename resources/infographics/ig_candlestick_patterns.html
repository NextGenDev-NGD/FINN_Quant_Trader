Candlestick Anatomy
Bullish
Bearish

Body — filled rectangle between open and close. Green/white = close > open (bullish). Red/black = close < open (bearish).

Upper wick — line above body to the period high. Long upper wick = price was rejected at the high.

Lower wick — line below body to the period low. Long lower wick = price was rejected at the low.

Long body = strong directional commitment. Short body = indecision. No opposing wick = no rejection from that extreme.

Single-Candle Patterns
PatternSignalBody & WicksMeaningContext Required
Bullish MarubozuBullishLarge bullish; no/minimal wicksComplete bull control; buyers dominated open to closeStrongest at base of consolidation or support
Bearish MarubozuBearishLarge bearish; no/minimal wicksComplete bear control; sellers dominated open to closeStrongest at top of rally or resistance
HammerBullish Rev.Small body at top of range; lower wick ≥ 2× body; minimal upper wickBuyers rejected a low — bullish reversal signalMust appear after a downtrend or at support. Identical shape to Hanging Man — position in trend determines meaning.
Hanging ManBearish Rev.Small body at top of range; lower wick ≥ 2× body; minimal upper wickSame shape as Hammer — at the top, signals sellers tested the lowMust appear after an uptrend or at resistance. Requires next-candle confirmation.
Inverted HammerBullish Rev.Small body at bottom of range; upper wick ≥ 2× body; minimal lower wickBuyers tested higher — potential reversal, needs confirmationMust appear after a downtrend. Identical shape to Shooting Star.
Shooting StarBearish Rev.Small body at bottom of range; upper wick ≥ 2× body; minimal lower wickSellers rejected a rally — bearish reversal signalMust appear after an uptrend or at resistance
DojiIndecisionNear-zero body (open ≈ close); wicks both sidesNeither side controls — balance between buyers and sellersSignificance depends on prior trend and what follows
Dragonfly DojiBullish Ind.Near-zero body at top of range; long lower wick; no upper wickBuyers recovered all losses — bullish indecisionStrongest at support after a downtrend
Gravestone DojiBearish Ind.Near-zero body at bottom of range; long upper wick; no lower wickSellers recovered all gains — bearish indecisionStrongest at resistance after an uptrend
Spinning TopIndecisionSmall body, centered; roughly equal wicks both sidesNeither side wins decisively — trend may be pausingContext and next candle determine meaning

Hammer vs. Hanging Man: Identical shape. Meaning is determined entirely by position in trend. Bottom of downtrend = Hammer (bullish). Top of uptrend = Hanging Man (bearish).

Inverted Hammer vs. Shooting Star: Same rule. Bottom of downtrend = Inverted Hammer (bullish). Top of uptrend = Shooting Star (bearish).

FINN Application Rules

Every candlestick pattern is a hypothesis, not a guarantee. A hammer at support does not mean the stock will rise — it means buying pressure was present at that level.

Context > Pattern. The same pattern at support in an uptrend has a higher probability than in the middle of a range. Always identify trend and level before identifying the candle.

Volume confirms. A reversal candle on above-average volume is significantly stronger than the same candle on below-average volume.

Run the IC test. Before using any pattern as a systematic entry signal, test it on your universe: does it predict positive forward returns with IC > 0.05 and p < 0.05 over a 3-year sample?
