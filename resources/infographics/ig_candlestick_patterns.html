Single-Candle Patterns
Bullish Marubozu
BullishSingle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bullish body; no or minimal wicks. Buyers controlled open to close completely.
Confirmation
Strongest at base of consolidation or support. Above-average volume preferred.
"Complete bull control — but confirm the level before treating as entry."
Bearish Marubozu
BearishSingle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bearish body; no or minimal wicks. Sellers controlled open to close completely.
Confirmation
Strongest at top of rally or resistance. Signals strong directional conviction.
"Complete bear control. Same rule: location first, pattern second."
Hammer
Bullish Rev.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Small body at top of range; lower wick ≥ 2× body; minimal upper wick. Buyers rejected a low.
Confirmation
Must appear after a downtrend or at support. Identical shape to Hanging Man — position in trend determines meaning.
"Same shape as Hanging Man. The trend tells you which one it is."
Hanging Man
Bearish Rev.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Same shape as Hammer — small body at top, long lower wick. At the top, sellers tested the low.
Confirmation
Must appear after an uptrend or at resistance. Requires next-candle confirmation (bearish close).
"Context is everything. Bottom of downtrend = Hammer. Top of uptrend = Hanging Man."
Inverted Hammer
Bullish Rev.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Small body at bottom of range; upper wick ≥ 2× body; minimal lower wick. Buyers tested higher.
Confirmation
Must appear after a downtrend. Identical shape to Shooting Star. Needs next-candle confirmation.
"Buyers tried and were partially rejected. Watch the next candle."
Shooting Star
Bearish Rev.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Small body at bottom of range; upper wick ≥ 2× body. Sellers rejected a rally from the high.
Confirmation
Must appear after an uptrend or at resistance. Same shape as Inverted Hammer — position determines meaning.
"Sellers slammed the high. Top of uptrend + this shape = watch for reversal."
Doji
IndecisionSingle
Details ▾
Formation
Near-zero body (open ≈ close); wicks on both sides. Neither buyers nor sellers controlled the session.
Confirmation
Significance depends on prior trend and next candle. A Doji after a long trend signals exhaustion — not a reversal on its own.
"Indecision is data. What happens next is the signal."
Dragonfly Doji
Bullish Ind.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Near-zero body at the top of range; long lower wick; no upper wick. Buyers recovered all losses from the session low.
Confirmation
Strongest at support after a downtrend. Long lower wick shows significant buying pressure at the low.
"Sellers tried. Buyers erased it all. That's the signal."
Gravestone Doji
Bearish Ind.Single
Details ▾
Formation
Near-zero body at bottom of range; long upper wick; no lower wick. Sellers recovered all gains from the session high.
Confirmation
Strongest at resistance after an uptrend. Long upper wick = buyers were rejected hard at the high.
"Buyers tried. Sellers erased it all. Mirror image of the Dragonfly."
Spinning Top
IndecisionSingle
Details ▾
Formation
Small body; roughly equal wicks on both sides. Neither side wins decisively — trend may be pausing.
Confirmation
Context and next candle determine meaning. Multiple spinning tops in a row = range-bound market.
"Indecision cluster = wait for resolution before trading."
Two-Candle Patterns
Bullish Engulfing
Bullish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Small bearish candle followed by a larger bullish candle whose body fully engulfs the prior body.
Confirmation
Next candle closes higher. Volume on the engulfing candle above prior candle volume — critical. At support.
"The engulfing candle with high volume is the signal. Low volume = suspect."
Bearish Engulfing
Bearish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Small bullish candle followed by a larger bearish candle whose body fully engulfs the prior body.
Confirmation
Next candle closes lower. Volume on the engulfing candle above prior. At resistance after uptrend.
"One of the most reliable reversal signals — when volume confirms."
Bullish Harami
Bullish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bearish candle followed by a small bullish candle that fits inside the prior body — "inside bar."
Confirmation
Less powerful than Engulfing. More of a pause signal — requires next-candle confirmation. Stronger at support.
"A pause, not a reversal on its own. Wait for confirmation."
Bearish Harami
Bearish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bullish candle followed by a small bearish candle that fits inside the prior body.
Confirmation
Requires next-candle confirmation. At resistance after uptrend. Weaker signal than Bearish Engulfing.
"Same principle: the pause needs confirmation before it's a reversal."
Tweezer Bottom
Bullish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Two candles with matching or near-matching lows. Double rejection of the same price level.
Confirmation
Strongest when second candle is bullish after a bearish first. At support. Volume expansion preferred.
"The market tested the low twice. Both times, buyers defended it."
Tweezer Top
Bearish Rev.Two-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Two candles with matching or near-matching highs at resistance. Double rejection of the same high.
Confirmation
Strongest when second candle is bearish after a bullish first. At resistance after uptrend.
"Sellers rejected the same high twice. That's a defended level."
Three-Candle Patterns
Morning Star
Bullish Rev.Three-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bearish → small indecision (gap or small body, lower) → large bullish closing into the first body.
Confirmation
Highest volume on the third candle. After a downtrend. Third candle should close at least 50% into the first body.
"Three-candle story: sellers, indecision, buyers win. Volume on candle 3 is the verdict."
Evening Star
Bearish Rev.Three-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Large bullish → small indecision at the top → large bearish closing into the first body. Mirror of Morning Star.
Confirmation
Highest volume on the third (bearish) candle. After an uptrend. Third candle closes at least 50% into first body.
"Buyers, indecision at the top, sellers win. The high volume bearish candle is the tell."
Three White Soldiers
Bullish Cont.Three-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Three consecutive large bullish candles, each opening within the prior body and closing near its own high.
Confirmation
Declining volume on the third candle can signal exhaustion — watch for stalling. Strongest after a downtrend base.
"Three strong bulls in a row is momentum — but check if the third has less volume."
Three Black Crows
Bearish Cont.Three-Candle
Details ▾
Formation
Three consecutive large bearish candles, each opening within the prior body and closing near its own low.
Confirmation
Declining volume on the third may indicate selling exhaustion. Strongest after an uptrend or at resistance breakdown.
"Three sessions of sustained selling. The trend is asserting itself."

Identical shapes, opposite meanings: Hammer (bullish) and Hanging Man (bearish) are the same candle. Inverted Hammer (bullish) and Shooting Star (bearish) are the same candle. The trend context before the candle — not the shape — determines the interpretation.

Volume is the confirming variable. A reversal candle on above-average volume is significantly stronger than the same candle on below-average volume. IC-test any pattern before systematic use: does it predict positive forward returns with IC > 0.05 and p < 0.05 over a 3-year sample?

"Every candlestick pattern is a hypothesis, not a guarantee. A hammer at support does not mean the stock will rise — it means buying pressure was present at that level. Context first. Level second. Pattern third. Volume confirms."
