The Four Heikin Ashi Formulas
HA Close
Close
HA_Close = (O + H + L + C) / 4
Average of the real candle's four prices. Smoother than the true close — reduces noise from end-of-bar spikes.
HA Open
Open
HA_Open = (prev HA_Open + prev HA_Close) / 2
Average of the previous HA candle's open and close. This is why HA candles chain together — each depends on the prior.
HA High
High
HA_High = max(H, HA_Open, HA_Close)
The highest of: real high, HA open, HA close. Often identical to the real high, but may differ when HA body is above actual wick.
HA Low
Low
HA_Low = min(L, HA_Open, HA_Close)
The lowest of: real low, HA open, HA close. Smoothed low that reduces false signal from transient wick extremes.

Reading HA Candles — Signal Guide
▲ Strong Uptrend Bullish
| ← Short or no upper wick ████ ████ ████ ← No lower wicks (key signal)
What it shows: Multiple consecutive green HA candles with no lower wicks. Bodies are large.
Signal: Momentum is strongly bullish. Buyers control every intrabar dip.
Action: Hold or add to long positions. Do not sell on minor dips.
▼ Strong Downtrend Bearish
← No upper wicks (key signal) ████ ████ ████ | ← Short or no lower wick
What it shows: Multiple consecutive red HA candles with no upper wicks.
Signal: Momentum is strongly bearish. Sellers dominate every intrabar rally.
Action: Hold or add to short positions. Avoid catching falling knives.
◇ Indecision / Pivot Caution
| ← Upper wick appears ─── ← Small body (doji-like) | ← Lower wick appears Small body with wicks on both sides
What it shows: Small HA body with wicks on both sides. Neither buyers nor sellers in control.
Signal: Potential trend reversal or consolidation ahead.
Action: Tighten stops, reduce size, wait for directional confirmation.
↑ Trend Resumption Continuation
─── ← Doji-like pause in uptrend ████ ← Large green resumes ████ ← Strong body, no lower wick Doji → large green = trend resumes
What it shows: Small HA candle (pause) followed by large body in original trend direction.
Signal: The trend has resumed after a minor pause — not a reversal.
Action: Re-enter or add to the trend position on the confirmation candle.
↕ Wick Appearance Warning Watch
████ ← Green candle | ← Lower wick appears for first time First lower wick in green sequence = buyers losing control intrabar
What it shows: In an uptrend, a lower wick appears on a green HA candle for the first time.
Signal: Sellers are beginning to push back during the session. Trend may be losing momentum.
Action: Tighten stop-loss. Don't exit yet — wait for a full candle color change.
⇄ Color Change = Reversal Signal Exit / Flip
████ ← Last green candle ─── ← Doji transition (optional) ████ ← First red candle = confirmation Color flip from green → red
What it shows: HA candles switch from green to red (or vice versa).
Signal: Trend has likely reversed. Exit the position in the prior trend direction.
Action: Exit on color change confirmation. Consider entering opposite trend.
FINN Rules — Heikin Ashi
Use HA as a filter, not a trigger
HA candles identify trend direction and momentum. Use them to filter trade direction (only take longs when HA is green with no lower wicks), then trigger entries using standard candles for precise timing.
No lower wick = strongest bull signal
A sequence of green HA candles with no lower wicks means buyers controlled every single intrabar dip. This is the visual signature of a strong trend. When lower wicks appear, momentum is fading.
Never use HA prices for order entry
HA open/close prices are modified — they are NOT real market prices. Never place stop-loss orders or limit orders at HA levels. Always convert back to standard candle prices for any order placement.
HA lags — price leads
Because HA averages prior prices, it introduces a lag. A trend reversal on standard candles may take 1–3 HA candles to become visible. Use standard candles to spot early warning signals before the HA color change.
Gaps are invisible on HA charts
The HA averaging formula eliminates gaps between sessions. If gap analysis is part of your strategy (e.g., gap-and-go setups), switch to standard candlesticks — HA will mask the information entirely.
Best on daily and weekly timeframes
Heikin Ashi works best on daily and weekly timeframes where the smoothing adds the most value. On intraday charts (5min, 15min), the lag becomes problematic and standard candles are preferable.
Known Limitations — And How to Work Around Them
Lag in signals
HA candles average prior data, so trend changes appear 1–3 candles late. You can miss optimal entries at reversals.
Workaround: Use standard candles on a lower timeframe to spot the reversal early, then confirm with HA on the higher timeframe.
Prices are not real
HA O/H/L/C are modified values. The "HA price" at 2:30pm is NOT what the market actually traded. Cannot be used for backtesting with real execution.
Workaround: Use standard OHLC for all backtests and order placement. HA is a visualization tool only.
Gaps hidden
Overnight and weekend gaps are smoothed out by the formula. Gap strategies, gap fills, and gap-and-go setups are invisible on HA charts.
Workaround: Run HA alongside standard candles (dual chart view) when gaps are relevant to your setup.
Poor in ranging markets
HA excels in trending markets. In choppy, sideways markets, HA candles flip color frequently, generating false trend signals.
Workaround: Check ADX > 25 (trending) before relying on HA signals. Below ADX 25, use standard candles or Bollinger Bands instead.
Python — Heikin Ashi Calculator + Filter
import yfinance as yf import pandas as pd def heikin_ashi(ticker, period="6mo"): df = yf.download(ticker, period=period, progress=False) df.columns = [c[0] if isinstance(c, tuple) else c for c in df.columns] ha = df.copy() # HA Close = average of real OHLC ha['HA_Close'] = (df['Open'] + df['High'] + df['Low'] + df['Close']) / 4 # HA Open = average of prior HA Open and HA Close (iterative) ha['HA_Open'] = 0.0 ha.iloc[0, ha.columns.get_loc('HA_Open')] = (df['Open'].iloc[0] + df['Close'].iloc[0]) / 2 for i in range(1, len(ha)): ha.iloc[i, ha.columns.get_loc('HA_Open')] = ( ha['HA_Open'].iloc[i-1] + ha['HA_Close'].iloc[i-1] ) / 2 # HA High and Low ha['HA_High'] = ha[['High', 'HA_Open', 'HA_Close']].max(axis=1) ha['HA_Low'] = ha[['Low', 'HA_Open', 'HA_Close']].min(axis=1) # Signal: is candle bullish with no lower wick? ha['HA_Bull'] = ha['HA_Close'] > ha['HA_Open'] ha['No_Lower_Wick'] = ha['HA_Low'] == ha['HA_Open'].where(ha['HA_Bull'], ha['HA_Close']) ha['Strong_Bull'] = ha['HA_Bull'] & ha['No_Lower_Wick'] print(f"\nLast 5 HA candles for {ticker}:") print(ha[['HA_Open', 'HA_High', 'HA_Low', 'HA_Close', 'Strong_Bull']].tail(5).round(2)) return ha # Example usage ha_df = heikin_ashi('SPY')
