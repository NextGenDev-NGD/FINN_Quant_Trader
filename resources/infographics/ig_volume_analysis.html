Core Principles — Volume as a Confirmation Tool
01
Volume confirms price
Price moves on high volume are more likely to sustain than moves on low volume. Volume is the fuel — price is the vehicle. No fuel = the move stalls.
Foundational
02
Divergence warns of reversal
When price makes a new high but volume is declining, the move is losing conviction. This divergence often precedes a reversal — traders are running out of buyers willing to push further.
Warning signal
03
Volume dries up = accumulation or exhaustion
Very low volume after a decline often signals that selling has exhausted itself — a base is forming. Very low volume after a rally signals complacency — potential top.
Context-dependent
04
Compare to the 20-day average
Volume only has meaning relative to its baseline. Always compare today's volume to the 20-day average volume. "High" and "low" volume are meaningless without this benchmark.
Measurement rule
Breakout Volume Patterns

High-Volume Breakout

Bullish confirmation
Price: ───────────────────────── / ← Breakout / Vol: ▂▂▃▂▃▂▂▂▃▂▂▂▂▂▂▂█████ ↑ Volume surges
What it is Price breaks through a key resistance level with volume ≥ 150% of the 20-day average.
Avg volume
1x
Breakout vol
≥1.5x
Signal Strong. Institutional participation confirmed. Breakout likely to follow through.
Action Enter on breakout bar or first retest of broken resistance. Set stop below the breakout level.
Invalidate Price closes back below the breakout level — exit immediately.

Low-Volume Breakout

Suspect — wait
Price: ───────────────────────── / ← Breakout / Vol: ▂▂▃▂▃▂▂▂▃▂▂▂▂▂▂▂▂▂ ↑ Volume stays low
What it is Price breaks a resistance level but volume is below average or only average — no conviction.
Avg volume
1x
Breakout vol
<1x
Signal Weak. High failure rate. Without volume, there are not enough buyers to sustain the move.
Action Wait for a volume-confirmed follow-through day before entering. Do not chase the initial move.
Risk These often resolve as bull traps. Price fades back below the breakout level.
Reversal Volume Patterns

Bearish Volume Divergence

Top warning
Price: /\/\ / \ / / \/ ← New high, lower volume Vol: ████ █████ ▃▂▃▂▂ ↑ ↑ Peak 1 Peak 2 Volume declining = fewer buyers
What it is Price makes a higher high but volume is lower than on the prior high. Buyers losing conviction.
Signal The advance is running out of buying pressure. Often precedes a top or reversal.
Action Tighten stops on existing longs. Do not add new positions. Watch for candlestick reversal confirmation.
Combined Strongest when paired with H&S or double-top pattern forming simultaneously.

Bullish Volume Divergence

Bottom warning
Price: \/\/ \ /\ \ ← New low, lower volume \ \/ Vol: ████ █████ ▃▂▃▂▂ ↑ ↑ Sellers losing strength = bottom near
What it is Price makes a lower low but volume is declining — sellers are exhausting themselves.
Signal Selling pressure weakening. Often appears at bottoms before reversals.
Action Watch for candlestick reversal at key support. Divergence alone is not an entry — wait for price confirmation.
Combined Strongest at Fibonacci support + double bottom + IH&S pattern forming.
On-Balance Volume (OBV)

On-Balance Volume — Accumulation / Distribution Indicator

Formula: OBV += Volume (up days) or −= Volume (down days)
How it works
Up day
OBV = OBV_prev + Volume (buying pressure added)
Down day
OBV = OBV_prev − Volume (selling pressure subtracted)
Flat day
OBV unchanged
Scale
Absolute value is meaningless — direction and divergence matter
Signals to watch
OBV rising + Price rising
Trend confirmed — strong buying conviction
OBV falling + Price rising
Bearish divergence — distribution is occurring, reversal risk high
OBV rising + Price flat/falling
Bullish divergence — smart money accumulating, price may lag
OBV flat + Price moving
Unclear — price move is not supported by volume flow
BULLISH DIVERGENCE: BEARISH DIVERGENCE: Price: ───────────────────── Price: ───────────────────────── /\ /\ ← lower lows /\ /\ ← higher highs / \ / \ / \ / OBV: ───────────────────── OBV: ───────────────────────── /\ /\ ← rising OBV /\ /\ ← falling OBV / \ / \ / \ / → OBV rising while price falls = accumulation (bullish) → OBV falling while price rises = distribution (bearish)
Climactic Volume — Exhaustion Signals
Selling Climax (Capitulation)
What it looks like: An extremely large red candle on massive volume — typically 3x–5x average — often with a long lower wick. Price gapped down or broke a major support level.
What it means: Every remaining seller has sold at once. There is no one left to sell — the selling is exhausted. This is capitulation.
What happens next: Price often reverses sharply or bases immediately after. The volume spike IS the bottom signal (not before it).
Action: Watch for the first green day following a selling climax. Volume should begin to normalize. Do not short into a selling climax — the risk/reward is extremely poor.
Caution: Climax does not guarantee instant reversal — price may retest the low (lower volume) before recovering.
Buying Climax (Blow-Off Top)
What it looks like: Extreme spike in volume on a massive green candle — everyone is rushing to buy at the top. Price accelerates above all moving averages with huge volume bars.
What it means: Every willing buyer has bought. Retail FOMO has been exhausted. There is no one left to push price higher — smart money is distributing into the buying frenzy.
What happens next: Price often reverses or stalls sharply. The blow-off top is a top-forming signal, not a continuation signal.
Action: If long, tighten stops significantly or take profits. Do not chase buy climax candles — you are buying at the top of a distribution. Watch for the first red reversal candle.
Caution: In very strong markets (earnings, news catalyst), climax volume can precede a brief pause, not a reversal. Context matters.

Python — Volume Analysis + OBV
import yfinance as yf import pandas as pd def volume_analysis(ticker, period="6mo"): df = yf.download(ticker, period=period, progress=False) df.columns = [c[0] if isinstance(c, tuple) else c for c in df.columns] # 20-day average volume baseline df['Vol_Avg20'] = df['Volume'].rolling(20).mean() df['Vol_Ratio'] = df['Volume'] / df['Vol_Avg20'] # Volume classification df['Vol_Signal'] = 'Normal' df.loc[df['Vol_Ratio'] >= 1.5, 'Vol_Signal'] = 'HIGH' df.loc[df['Vol_Ratio'] >= 3.0, 'Vol_Signal'] = 'CLIMAX' df.loc[df['Vol_Ratio'] <= 0.5, 'Vol_Signal'] = 'DRY-UP' # OBV calculation df['Direction'] = df['Close'].diff().apply(lambda x: 1 if x > 0 else (-1 if x < 0 else 0)) df['OBV'] = (df['Volume'] * df['Direction']).cumsum() # Price vs OBV divergence price_trend = df['Close'].iloc[-1] > df['Close'].iloc[-20] obv_trend = df['OBV'].iloc[-1] > df['OBV'].iloc[-20] if price_trend and obv_trend: divergence = "CONFIRMED — Price and OBV both rising" elif price_trend and not obv_trend: divergence = "BEARISH DIVERGENCE — Price up, OBV down (distribution)" elif not price_trend and obv_trend: divergence = "BULLISH DIVERGENCE — Price down, OBV up (accumulation)" else: divergence = "Both declining — confirmed downtrend" print(f"\n=== Volume Analysis: {ticker} ===") print(f"OBV Divergence: {divergence}\n") print(df[['Close', 'Volume', 'Vol_Ratio', 'Vol_Signal', 'OBV']].tail(10).round(2)) return df # Example usage volume_analysis('SPY')
