What Is a Gap
Gap-Up and Gap-Down — Price Discontinuity
Gap-Up Example
Day 2 open: $52.00  ┌──┐
                    │  │ ← Day 2 candle opens above
════════════════════════ GAP (empty price range: $49–$52)
Day 1 close: $49.00 ┌──┐
                    │  │ ← Day 1 candle closed here
Gap size = ($52 - $49) / $49 = 6.1%
Gap-Down Example
Day 1 close: $49.00 ┌──┐
                    │  │ ← Day 1 candle closed here
════════════════════════ GAP (empty price range: $44–$49)
Day 2 open: $44.00  ┌──┐
                    │  │ ← Day 2 candle opens below
Gap size = ($49 - $44) / $49 = 10.2%

A gap is a zone of price where no trading occurred. The prior close and next open do not connect. Gaps can fill later (price returns to the gap zone) or remain open indefinitely.

The Four Gap Types
Common Gap
Fills Quickly
What it is: A small gap that occurs in the middle of normal price action with no significant catalyst. Most often seen in consolidation ranges or low-volatility periods.
Why it happens: Overnight futures drift, thin pre-market order books, minor overnight news.
Typical Size
< 0.5%
Volume
Normal or below
Fill Probability
High — often same day
Signal Value
Low — ignore
Breakaway Gap
Trend Signal
What it is: A gap that launches price out of a consolidation or base pattern, marking the beginning of a new trend. This is a high-conviction signal that new institutional money is entering.
Why it happens: Earnings surprise, major news catalyst, breakout from a long-consolidation base on high volume.
Typical Size
2–8%+
Volume
Significantly above avg
Fill Probability
Low — gap often holds
Signal Value
High — trend starting
Runaway Gap
Continuation
What it is: A gap that occurs in the middle of an established trend — price has already been moving in one direction, and this gap marks an acceleration. Also called a "measuring gap" because it often occurs near the midpoint of the total move.
Why it happens: Momentum is strong; late-entering institutional players adding to positions.
Typical Size
1–4%
Volume
Above average
Fill Probability
Moderate — fills eventually
Signal Value
Confirms trend strength
Exhaustion Gap
Reversal Warning
What it is: A gap that occurs near the end of a trend, representing the final burst of momentum before the trend reverses. Looks similar to a runaway gap but is followed by a failed continuation and high-volume reversal.
Why it happens: The last retail buyers pile in after a long trend. Smart money is distributing into this buying pressure.
Typical Size
2–5%+
Volume
Very high — climactic
Fill Probability
High — fills as trend ends
Signal Value
Reversal warning signal

FINN Code Reference — Gap Detection
# Gap detection in FINN backtests df['gap_pct'] = (df['Open'] - df['Close'].shift(1)) / df['Close'].shift(1) * 100 # Gap-up and gap-down flags df['gap_up'] = (df['gap_pct'] > 0.5).astype(int) df['gap_down'] = (df['gap_pct'] < -0.5).astype(int) # Large gap flag (breakaway / exhaustion candidates) df['large_gap_up'] = (df['gap_pct'] > 2.0).astype(int) df['large_gap_down'] = (df['gap_pct'] < -2.0).astype(int) # Gap fill detection (did price return to the gap zone during the day?) df['gap_filled'] = ( (df['gap_up'] == 1) & (df['Low'] <= df['Close'].shift(1)) ).astype(int)

You cannot identify gap type until after the gap. A breakaway and exhaustion gap look identical on the day they occur. The difference is confirmed by what happens over the next 2–5 sessions. Treat all large gaps as unclassified on day 1 — classify after observing the follow-through.

Volume is the primary classifier. A large gap on 3× average volume is a real signal. A large gap on below-average volume in a low-liquidity pre-market session is noise.

Gaps fill — eventually. A commonly cited market observation is that most gaps eventually fill. In practice: common gaps fill quickly, breakaway gaps may take months or years to fill, and exhaustion gaps fill as part of the reversal. Do not trade "gap fill" as a stand-alone strategy without IC validation.
