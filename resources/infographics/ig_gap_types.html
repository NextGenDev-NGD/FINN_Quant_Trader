Gap Anatomy — Price Discontinuity
Common Gap Fills Quickly
What it is: A small gap in normal price action. No significant catalyst. Most common in consolidation ranges and low-volatility periods.
Typical Size< 0.5%
Fill ProbabilityHIGH — 90%
Signal ValueLOW — Ignore
Volume on Gap DayNormal or Below
FINN Analysis ▼
Don't trade it. Common gaps occur because of overnight futures drift or thin pre-market liquidity — they have no predictive value for the next move. They fill quickly because the gap had no institutional driver behind it. Filter these out of your gap detection script by requiring >0.5% gap and above-average volume.
Breakaway Gap Trend Signal
What it is: Launches price out of a consolidation or base. Marks the start of a new trend. High-conviction signal — new institutional money entering.
Typical Size2–8%+
Fill ProbabilityLOW — 15%
Signal ValueHIGH — Trend Start
Volume on Gap Day3–5× Average
FINN Analysis ▼
High-priority signal. The gap zone acts as support after the breakout — it's the new floor. Best entry: buy the gap or buy the first retest of the gap zone. Stop: below the bottom of the gap zone (a close below = thesis broken). Volume is the validator — if volume is not 2× average or above, treat it with skepticism. Catalysts: earnings surprise, major news, sector leadership change.
Runaway Gap Continuation
What it is: Occurs mid-trend — price already moving, gap marks acceleration. Also called a "measuring gap" — often appears near the midpoint of the total move.
Typical Size1–4%
Fill ProbabilityMODERATE — 45%
Signal ValueMEDIUM — Trend Confirms
Volume on Gap Day2–3× Average
FINN Analysis ▼
Confirms the trend, estimates the target. The "measuring" property: if the trend began at $40 and the runaway gap is at $50, expect the total move to end around $60 (the gap is near the midpoint). Hold existing positions. Entering new positions is riskier — you are mid-trend, not at the start. Watch for the exhaustion gap to follow in the same direction. That's the exit signal.
Exhaustion Gap Reversal Warning
What it is: Final burst of momentum near trend end. Looks like a runaway gap — confirmed only by failed continuation and high-volume reversal that follows.
Typical Size2–5%+
Fill ProbabilityHIGH — 80%
Signal ValueHIGH — Reversal
Volume on Gap Day4–6× Average (Climax)
FINN Analysis ▼
You cannot identify it on day 1. An exhaustion gap and a runaway gap look identical on the gap day. Classification requires 2–5 sessions of follow-through (or lack of it). Signal: price fills back into the gap zone within 1–3 days on volume. Action: if long — tighten stops aggressively or close position. Do not short into the gap on day 1 — the risk/reward is poor before confirmation.

FINN Code Reference — Gap Detection
# Gap detection in FINN backtests df['gap_pct'] = (df['Open'] - df['Close'].shift(1)) / df['Close'].shift(1) * 100 # Gap-up and gap-down flags (filter noise with 0.5% threshold) df['gap_up'] = (df['gap_pct'] > 0.5).astype(int) df['gap_down'] = (df['gap_pct'] < -0.5).astype(int) # Large gap flag (breakaway / exhaustion candidates) df['large_gap_up'] = (df['gap_pct'] > 2.0).astype(int) df['large_gap_down'] = (df['gap_pct'] < -2.0).astype(int) # Volume classification (add after Vol_Avg20 is calculated) df['gap_high_vol'] = ( ((df['gap_up'] == 1) | (df['gap_down'] == 1)) & (df['Volume'] >= df['Vol_Avg20'] * 2.0) ).astype(int) # Gap fill detection (did price return to the gap zone during the day?) df['gap_filled'] = ( (df['gap_up'] == 1) & (df['Low'] <= df['Close'].shift(1)) ).astype(int)
You cannot classify a gap on the day it occurs. A breakaway and an exhaustion gap are identical on day 1 — large size, high volume, strong directional move. The difference is the follow-through. Wait 2–5 sessions. If price holds above the gap zone — breakaway. If price fills back through it — exhaustion. Volume is the primary classifier; story and catalyst are secondary.
