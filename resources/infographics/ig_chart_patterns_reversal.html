Cup & Handle
Bullish Rev. Prior Trend: Uptrend
Structure · Volume · Target · Failure ▾
Structure
Prior uptrend → rounded U-shaped decline (cup) → tight high-level consolidation (handle). Handle must form in the upper 50% of the cup and slope downward mildly. Cup depth: 15–30% ideal. Handle: 5–12%.
Duration
Cup: 3–6 months (ideal). Handle: 1–4 weeks. Works on shorter timeframes proportionally.

Invalidation: Sharp V-recovery (not a rounding base); new lower low during the base phase
"Best when sector rotation or a fundamental catalyst exists. Volume gradually increasing on the right arc is the early tell — before the breakout."

The neckline is the support/resistance line formed by connecting the intermediate troughs (in Double Top, H&S) or peaks (in Double Bottom, IH&S). It is not just a visual marker — it is the trade trigger line. Enter only on a confirmed close beyond the neckline, not on an intraday touch. Neckline retests (price returns to test the broken neckline from the other side) are the highest-probability entry points after a breakout. If the retest fails — price breaks back through the neckline — exit the position. The pattern has failed.

Pattern Anatomy — Key Components
Peak / Trough
Pivot Point
The key high or low that defines the pattern. Must be a clear, discrete swing — not a gradual drift.
Neckline
Trade Trigger
Line connecting intermediate swings. Break of neckline = entry signal. Retest = confirmation entry.
Pattern Height
Target Input
Distance from neckline to the most extreme peak/trough. Projects the minimum expected move after breakout.
Right Side
Quality Check
Right shoulder or second peak/trough should show declining volume vs. the left side — this signals exhaustion.
Breakout Bar
Volume Signal
The breakout candle should have volume ≥ 50% above the 20-day average. Low-volume breaks fail often.
Handle
C&H Specific
Tight consolidation after the cup's right lip. Must stay in the upper half of the cup — low handles weaken the setup.

FINN Rules — Reversal Patterns
Rule 01
Wait for the close
Intraday neckline touches are noise. Only a close on the relevant timeframe beyond the neckline counts as a trigger. Entering on a wick is anticipating the pattern, not trading it.
Rule 02
Volume must confirm
A neckline break on below-average volume has a high failure rate. If volume doesn't spike on the breakout candle, step aside and wait. No volume = no signal.
Rule 03
Target ≠ guarantee
The measured-move target is a probability zone, not a certainty. Take partial profits at 50–75% of target and trail a stop on the remainder. Don't count on hitting the exact level.
Rule 04
Symmetry matters
A visually clean, symmetric pattern (equal peaks, clean arc) has higher reliability than a forced or lopsided one. If you have to argue the pattern into existence — it isn't there.
Rule 05
Context over pattern
A bearish reversal pattern in a strong bull market has lower odds than the same pattern in a weakening sector. Pattern + context = signal. Pattern alone = noise.
Rule 06
Log invalidations
When a pattern fails (price reverses back through the neckline), log it as a failed hypothesis. Wrong predictions are data — they refine your read of market structure over time.

"A pattern on a chart is a description of what happened — not a prediction of what will happen. The neckline break with volume is the market voting. Until that vote is cast, the pattern is a working hypothesis. Run the IC test before you systematize any reversal signal: does it predict positive forward returns with IC > 0.05 over a 3-year sample? If not, it is art, not edge."
