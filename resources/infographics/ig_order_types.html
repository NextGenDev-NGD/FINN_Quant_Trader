Core Order Types
Market Order
✓ Execution✗ PriceFINN Use
Buys or sells immediately at the best available price. You accept whatever the market offers at the moment of submission.
Use when: Highly liquid stock (SPY, AAPL, QQQ); speed of entry matters more than getting a specific price; closing a position quickly.
Limit Order
✗ Execution✓ PriceFINN Use
Buy limit: fills at your price or lower. Sell limit: fills at your price or higher. Will not fill if price never reaches your level.
Use when: You have a specific entry price; stock is less liquid; you're willing to miss the trade to get your price.
Stop-Loss Order
✓ Execution (triggered)✗ PriceFINN Use
Becomes a market order when price hits your stop level. Protects against catastrophic loss. Does not guarantee the stop price as the fill price.
Use when: Protecting an open position. Every FINN trade must have a stop defined at entry.
Stop-Limit Order
✗ Execution✗ Price
Becomes a limit order when price hits the stop level. Neither execution nor fill price is guaranteed — if price blows through the limit, the order may not fill at all.
Use when: You want to avoid a bad fill after the stop triggers, but understand you may hold through a crash if price gaps past both levels.
Trailing Stop
✓ Execution (triggered)✗ PriceFINN Use
Stop level moves with the market as price rises (for longs). Locks in gains as price advances; triggers if price reverses by the trailing distance.
Use when: Riding a trend and want to protect accumulated gains without a fixed target. Set in $ or % from high.
OCO (One-Cancels-Other)
FINN Use
Two orders placed simultaneously. When one fills, the other is automatically cancelled. Typically: profit target (limit) + stop-loss (stop) placed at entry.
Use when: Setting both a profit target and a stop at entry — the standard FINN paper trading exit structure.
Advanced & Time-Based Order Types
Bracket Order
FINN Standard
An entry order paired with an automatic OCO (profit target + stop). All three legs are live from the moment the entry fills. Complete risk definition at entry.
Use when: Entering any planned FINN paper trade. The bracket structure enforces rule adherence automatically.
GTC (Good-til-Cancelled)
FINN Use
Order stays active until filled or manually cancelled (broker max: 60–90 days). Does not expire at day's end.
Use when: Waiting for a specific price level that may take multiple sessions to reach. Review GTC orders regularly — market conditions change.
Day Order
Expires at market close if not filled during that session. The default duration for most retail broker orders.
Use when: Your setup is time-sensitive and only valid for today's session. Default choice for most intraday entries.
Market-on-Open (MOO)
✓ Execution
Executes at the opening auction price. Submitted before the open; joins the opening auction pool.
Use when: You want the opening print; useful for gap setups you've pre-planned overnight.
Market-on-Close (MOC)
✓ Execution
Executes at the closing auction price. Must be submitted before the MOC cutoff (typically 3:45–3:55 PM ET).
Use when: End-of-day signals; rebalancing; you want exposure to the closing print and institutional settlement flow.
Extended Hours Order
Wide Spreads
Executes in pre-market or after-hours sessions. Available on Webull. Liquidity is thin; spreads are wide; fills may not represent real market-hours prices.
Use when: Paper trading practice only. In live trading — be cautious; results are not representative of regular-session execution.

The gap problem: A stock closes at $50. Bad earnings overnight. It opens at $42. Your $47 stop-loss becomes a market order that fills at $42 — not $47. You lost $8/share instead of $3. Stop-Limit orders avoid this by refusing to fill below $47 — but if the stock opens at $42, your limit at $47 never triggers. You hold through the gap with no protection. There is no order type that guarantees both execution and price through a gap.

FINN standard entry structure: Bracket order = entry (market or limit) + profit target (limit) + stop-loss (stop). All three legs defined before submission. No manual exits required — the bracket handles both scenarios automatically.

Stop placement rule: Set stops at ATR-based levels, not round numbers or "just below" the obvious support. A stop at $49.85 (1.5× ATR below the zone) is harder to hunt than a stop at $50.00.
