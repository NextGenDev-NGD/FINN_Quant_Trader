FINN — Quantitative Trading

6 Non-Negotiable Risk Rules

These rules are not suggestions — breaking any one of them can end a trading career
Tier 2 · FINN-Specific

Risk management is not what you do after a trade goes wrong. It is what determines your position size, your stop placement, and your daily exposure limit before you enter a single trade.

All 6 rules below apply on every trade, every day, without exception. There is no "this setup is so good I'll skip rule 3." That thinking is how accounts are blown.
The 6 Rules
1
2% Max Risk Per Trade
Never risk more than 2% of total account on any single position
Hard Limit
Risk ≤ 2% of account/trade
Position size = (Account × 0.02) ÷ (Entry − Stop). This calculation is non-negotiable. A conviction narrative is not a risk override.
Why: At 2% risk, you can lose 50 consecutive trades and still have 36% of your capital. At 10%, 10 consecutive losses = 65% loss. Recovery from 65% down requires +186% return.
2
6% Daily Drawdown Limit
Stop trading for the day if total losses reach 6% of account
Daily Kill Switch
Stop if daily P&L ≤ −6%
When you hit −6% on the day, close all positions and stop trading. No revenge trades. The market will be open tomorrow; your capital may not be.
Why: Bad days cluster. Continuing to trade after 3 consecutive losses compounds emotional decision-making. A forced break protects capital from revenge trading.
3
ATR-Based Stop Placement
Stops are set using ATR — not round numbers, not support lines alone
Stop Formula
Stop = Entry − (1.5 × ATR14)
Place stops 1.5–2× ATR beyond the entry. This accounts for normal price noise without giving up too much per trade. Never use round numbers as stop levels.
Why: Round-number stops get hunted. ATR-based stops are calibrated to actual price volatility, reducing the probability of stop-hunt wipeouts before the trade has time to work.
4
Minimum 2:1 R:R Required
Do not enter any trade with a risk-to-reward ratio below 2:1
Entry Gate
Target ≥ Entry + (2 × Risk)
Calculate target and stop before entering. If Target / Risk < 2.0, skip the trade. A setup with a great narrative and a 1:1 R:R is still a bad trade.
Why: With a 50% win rate and 2:1 R:R, you have positive expectancy. At 1:1, you need >50% win rate just to break even after commissions. Do the math before every trade.
5
Max 20% Sector Concentration
No more than 20% of capital in any single sector at any time
Concentration Limit
Sector exposure ≤ 20%
Track sector allocation across all open positions. Technology, healthcare, energy — no single sector should represent more than 20% of total deployed capital.
Why: Sector shocks (earnings, regulation, macro) hit correlated stocks simultaneously. If 60% of your capital is in tech and NVDA gaps down 15%, the correlation damage is catastrophic.
6
Document Every Trade
No trade exists if it is not logged in all_trades.csv before and after execution
Documentation Standard
Log entry + exit + rationale
Before entry: record thesis, entry, stop, target, position size. After exit: record outcome, what was right, what was wrong, what to do differently.
Why: Undocumented trades produce no learning. The log is not paperwork — it is the feedback loop that separates a trader who improves from one who repeats the same mistake with different tickers.
Common Rule Violations — What They Look Like
RuleViolation PatternHow It Ends
Rule 1 "This setup is different" → sizing up to 5–10% of account on a strong conviction trade One gap-down wipes out 20+ winning trades. Account permanently impaired.
Rule 2 After 3 losing trades: "I need to make it back today" → taking 4th, 5th, 6th trade Emotional state compounds losses. −6% becomes −15% in one session.
Rule 3 Placing stop at the obvious swing low or round number ($50.00, $100.00) Stop hunted. Price dips to $49.95, fills the stop, then reverses to the original target.
Rule 4 Entering a 1:1 trade because "it's a sure thing" or "the setup looks perfect" Even a 60% win rate at 1:1 R:R barely covers commissions. Slow account bleed.
Rule 5 Running 4 tech trades simultaneously during an earnings season One sector rotation or macro event hits all 4 at once. Correlation = full loss on entire position block.
Rule 6 "I'll log it later" → never logging it → repeating the same mistake next week No feedback loop. Same biases, same mistakes, indefinitely. Improvement is impossible without data.
⏹
Account-Level Kill Switch — Stop Trading Immediately If:
  • Daily loss exceeds 6% of account (Rule 2 trigger)
  • Monthly drawdown exceeds 15% of account start value
  • Live IC drops below 0.03 for 2 consecutive months (signal decay — return to backtesting)
  • You take a trade without calculating position size first
  • You deviate from the system during an emotional state (angry, scared, euphoric)
FINN

"Risk management is not about preventing losses — you will have losses. It is about making sure that a string of losses cannot end your ability to trade. Every rule above exists because someone, at some point, broke it and paid with their account. The rules are not constraints on your edge. They are what keeps you in the game long enough for your edge to compound."
