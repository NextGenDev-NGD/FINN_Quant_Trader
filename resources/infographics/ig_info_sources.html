Information Reliability Hierarchy
1
Price & Volume
The market's actual revealed preferences — what participants actually did with real money. Always primary. No interpretation required.
Lag: Real-time
2
Scheduled Macro Data
CPI, FOMC, NFP, GDP — high impact, predictable timing. Prepare for it. These move markets regardless of any technical signal.
Lag: None (scheduled)
3
Sentiment Indicators
VIX, P/C ratio, AAII, Fear & Greed. Useful at extremes; filter noise near the middle. Best as regime context, not entry timing.
Lag: Hours–Days
4
News Headlines
Directional input only. The market's reaction to news matters more than the news itself. A stock dropping on good news is a bearish signal.
Lag: Minutes–Hours
5
Analyst Opinions & Social Media
Noise until quantified. FINN uses FinBERT to extract signal from text; raw opinion is not a trading input. Treat as awareness only.
Lag: Varies / Unreliable
FINN DMB Morning Information Workflow
Step 1
Finviz Futures
Check ES, NQ, YM overnight direction. Note magnitude. Set directional bias.
⏱ 2 min
Step 2
Investing.com Calendar
Any high-impact macro events today? Earnings? Fed speakers? Flag and plan around them.
⏱ 2 min
Step 3
Finviz News
Scan overnight headlines. Any watchlist ticker touched? Any sector-wide event?
⏱ 3 min
Step 4
Finviz Heatmap
Broad move or sector rotation? Identify which sectors are leading and lagging.
⏱ 1 min
Step 5
TradingView
Check VIX level. Review watchlist charts. Identify gaps. Mark key levels on active setups.
⏱ 10 min
Step 6
DMB Entry
Write hypothesis: "Today I expect ___ because ___. I will watch for ___ to confirm/deny."
⏱ 3 min

More information is not more edge. A trader who checks 15 sources every morning is not better informed — they are more confused. The hierarchy above exists for a reason: price and volume are authoritative. Everything else is context. Spend 80% of your information time on primary sources and 20% on context.

The market's reaction is the signal, not the news. When CPI prints hotter than expected and the market rallies — the rally is the signal. The news is the input. Trade what the market does with information, not the information itself.
