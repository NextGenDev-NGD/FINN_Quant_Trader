What Paper Trading Is (and Isn't)
What it IS
  • Simulated trading with virtual money using real market prices
  • Real order types, real fills based on live bid/ask
  • Real P&L tracking — gains and losses are calculated accurately
  • Real psychological pressure — treat every decision as if the money is real
  • The primary training environment for the entire FINN course
What it ISN'T
  • Not risk-free training — bad habits formed here carry into live trading
  • Not identical to live — slippage and market impact are not fully simulated
  • Not an excuse for sloppy execution — the rules apply exactly as in live trading
  • Not a game — if you don't take it seriously, the live transition will be costly
FINN Paper Trading Rules
  • Starting capital: $100,000 paper (Webull default)
  • Max risk per trade: 2% of total portfolio = $2,000
  • Max positions at once: 3–5 concurrent
  • Stop-loss: Required on every trade — no exceptions
  • Journal entry: Required before and after every trade
  • Deviations: Must be logged, never silently accepted
Account Setup — Step by Step
Download Webull and Create Account
Download the Webull app (iOS or Android) or visit webull.com. Create a free account — no real money required for paper trading. Email + password only at this stage.
Access Paper Trading
In the app: tap the bottom center icon (Markets/Trade) → scroll to find "Paper Trading" or tap your profile icon → Paper Trading. On desktop: top navigation → Paper Trading.
Confirm Starting Capital
Default paper account starts with $1,000,000. FINN uses $100,000 to simulate a realistic account size. In paper trading settings, you can reset the account balance — set it to $100,000 before your first trade.
Enable Margin (for Short Selling)
Paper trading accounts include simulated margin by default. Short selling is available without additional setup. Confirm margin is enabled in: Paper Account → Settings → Account Type.
Configure Watchlist
Add your FINN watchlist tickers to Webull: tap Watchlist → + → Search symbol → Add. Mirror the watchlist structure from your TradingView setup for consistency.

Bracket Order Setup in Webull
Find the stock
Search ticker → tap Trade (bottom of stock page)
Set order type
Select Limit for the entry. Enter your calculated entry price.
Calculate and enter quantity
Quantity = (Account × 2% risk) ÷ (Entry − Stop). Round down to whole shares.
Enable TP/SL (Take Profit / Stop Loss)
Toggle TP/SL in the order form → enter your stop price and target price. This creates the OCO bracket automatically.
Review and submit
Confirm all three prices: entry, stop, target. Confirm quantity and max risk. Tap Submit.

FINN P&L Analysis — What to Record
Per-Trade Record
  • Entry date/time and price
  • Exit date/time and price
  • Planned stop vs. actual exit price
  • Planned target vs. actual exit price
  • Realized P&L ($ and % of account)
  • Rule adherence (followed / deviated)
  • Signal that triggered entry
Weekly Aggregate
  • Total trades this week
  • Win rate (wins ÷ total trades)
  • Average win vs. average loss (R-ratio)
  • Total P&L vs. SPY total return same week
  • Rule adherence rate (% of trades fully rule-compliant)
  • Cost of deviations ($ impact of all rule breaks)
Common Webull Paper Gotchas
  • Extended hours fills — paper trading may fill orders in pre/after hours at prices that wouldn't execute in real conditions. Flag these.
  • Stop-loss slippage — paper fills at the stop price exactly. Live trading fills at market price after the stop triggers — expect worse.
  • Reset temptation — never reset the account to hide losses. The drawdown is data. Own it.
  • Oversizing — paper money feels free. Enforce the 2% risk rule on every trade, regardless.

The single most important Webull paper trading rule: Treat every paper trade as if the money is real. The habit you build now is the habit you will execute with when real capital is at risk. A student who takes paper trading casually will fail in live trading for exactly the same reasons they were casual in paper trading. The money is virtual. The decisions are not.

Webull is your execution platform. TradingView is your analysis platform. Do your chart analysis, indicator work, and level-drawing in TradingView. Execute in Webull. Keep the tools separate — switching between them forces a deliberate pause between analysis and action, which reduces impulsive entries.
