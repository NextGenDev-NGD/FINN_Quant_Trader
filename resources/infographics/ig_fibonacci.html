How to Draw — 4-Step Protocol
01
Identify the significant swing
Find a clear, impulsive move: a strong rally (uptrend) or strong decline (downtrend). The move should be obvious on the chart — not a minor wiggle. The bigger and cleaner the swing, the more reliable the levels.
02
Anchor at swing extremes
Place the tool at the swing low → high for uptrend retracements. Place at swing high → low for downtrend. Use wick extremes (not candle bodies) as anchor points. This sets the 0% and 100% levels.
03
Wait for price to retrace
After anchoring, wait — do not trade the Fibonacci level until price reaches it AND shows a reaction. A level alone is not an entry signal. Look for: candlestick reversal pattern at the level, volume spike, momentum divergence.
04
Look for confluence
The best Fibonacci entries occur when multiple signals converge at the same level: Fib level + prior support/resistance + moving average + volume. Each additional signal raises probability. One signal alone = noise.
FINN Rules — Fibonacci
61.8% is the highest-probability level
The golden ratio level is where institutional algo orders cluster. If price reaches 61.8% and holds, it is often a high-quality entry in the direction of the prior trend. Treat it as your primary watch level.
Confluence makes the trade
A Fibonacci level by itself is not an edge. Fib level + S&R zone + candlestick signal + volume reaction = tradeable confluence. Stack the signals before entering.
Don't anchor to noise swings
Fibonacci levels are only as reliable as the swing you anchor them to. A valid swing is impulsive, clearly directional, and visible on your primary timeframe without zooming in excessively.
Timeframe determines strength
A 61.8% on a weekly chart is more powerful than a 61.8% on a 5-minute chart. Draw Fibonacci on your analysis timeframe (weekly or daily) and execute entries on a lower timeframe for precision.
Invalidate cleanly
If price closes through the 100% level (below the swing low in an uptrend), the retracement has become a reversal. Exit the trade — don't rationalize. Close beyond 100% is a hard invalidation.
Extensions tell you where to exit
Use extensions (127.2%, 161.8%, 200%) as profit targets, not entries. When a retracement entry works, these levels give you data-backed exits instead of guessing. 161.8% is the primary target; scale out in thirds.
Confluence Combos — What to Stack

Fib + Support/Resistance

If a prior S&R zone lines up within 0.5% of a Fibonacci level, the zone is doubly validated. Both institutional buyers (algo Fib orders) and manual traders (S&R) are watching the same price.

Fib level + Prior S/R zone = High-priority watch

Fib + Moving Average

A Fibonacci level that aligns with the 20-EMA, 50-SMA, or 200-SMA creates a strong dynamic support. Price often bounces off the Fib zone because the MA reinforces it simultaneously.

Fib level + 20/50/200 MA = Dynamic confluence

Fib + Volume Reaction

When price reaches a Fib level and volume dries up (exhaustion of selling in an uptrend), then spikes as price bounces, this is the cleanest signal that institutional demand is present at that exact level.

Fib level reached + Volume dries + Volume spike on bounce = Entry signal

Fib + Candlestick Pattern

A hammer, bullish engulfing, or doji at a Fibonacci level provides visual confirmation that a reversal is occurring at that precise price. This is the most direct entry timing signal.

Fib level + Hammer/Engulfing = Triggered entry
Python — Fibonacci Calculator
import yfinance as yf import pandas as pd def fib_levels(ticker, period="6mo"): df = yf.download(ticker, period=period, progress=False) df.columns = [c[0] if isinstance(c, tuple) else c for c in df.columns] swing_high = df['High'].max() swing_low = df['Low'].min() diff = swing_high - swing_low # Retracement levels (from high, pulling back toward low) levels = { '0.0% (High)' : swing_high, '23.6%' : swing_high - 0.236 * diff, '38.2%' : swing_high - 0.382 * diff, '50.0%' : swing_high - 0.500 * diff, '61.8% ★ Golden': swing_high - 0.618 * diff, '78.6%' : swing_high - 0.786 * diff, '100% (Low)' : swing_low, # Extensions '127.2% Ext' : swing_low - 0.272 * diff, '161.8% Ext ★' : swing_low - 0.618 * diff, } print(f"\n=== Fibonacci Levels: {ticker} ===") print(f"Swing High: ${swing_high:.2f} | Swing Low: ${swing_low:.2f}\n") for label, price in levels.items(): print(f" {label:<20} ${price:.2f}") return pd.Series(levels, name=ticker) # Example usage fib_levels('SPY')
