The 8 Trade Types
Position Trading
FINN Starting Point
Horizon
Weeks–Months
Min Capital
$1,000+
Difficulty
Level 1
What it is: Holding positions for weeks to months based on fundamental or macro thesis. The slowest style — decisions happen weekly or monthly, not daily.

Edge source: Trend identification, fundamental valuation, macro regime analysis.
Risk: Overnight and weekend exposure; large drawdowns possible in adverse regimes. Requires patience to hold through noise.
Swing Trading
FINN Primary Style
Horizon
2–10 Days
Min Capital
$2,000+
Difficulty
Level 2
What it is: Capturing price moves over 2–10 days. Uses technical setups (S&R, patterns, signals) on daily/4H charts. The dominant style in FINN's systematic approach.

Edge source: Technical signal IC, multi-signal composite scoring, backtested entry/exit rules.
Risk: Overnight gaps; news events during hold period. Position sizing and stop discipline are critical.
Trend Following
Horizon
Weeks–Months
Min Capital
$5,000+
Difficulty
Level 2
What it is: Systematic approach that buys strength and sells weakness across asset classes. No prediction — follows momentum until it ends. Classic quant strategy.

Edge source: Momentum factor (documented across 200+ years). Works best on diversified portfolios.
Risk: Long drawdown periods during choppy/mean-reverting markets. Requires rules-based discipline — no discretion.
Mean Reversion
Horizon
1–5 Days
Min Capital
$3,000+
Difficulty
Level 3
What it is: Betting that price reverts to a mean after an extreme move. Buy oversold; sell overbought. Counter-trend by nature — trades against short-term momentum.

Edge source: Statistical reversion of extreme readings (RSI, Bollinger Band z-score). Works best in range-bound markets.
Risk: Trend continuation destroys mean-reversion positions. Requires tight stops and regime filtering.
Day Trading
Horizon
Minutes–Hours
Min Capital
$25,000 (PDT)
Difficulty
Level 3
What it is: All positions opened and closed within the same trading day. No overnight exposure. Requires full-time attention during market hours.

PDT Rule: In the US, more than 3 day trades in a 5-session rolling window requires $25,000 in a margin account.
Risk: High transaction costs, spread costs, and emotional load. Most retail day traders underperform. Not the FINN starting point.
Options Trading
Horizon
Days–Weeks
Min Capital
$2,000+
Difficulty
Level 4
What it is: Trading contracts that give the right (not obligation) to buy/sell at a specified price by a specified date. Requires understanding of direction, timing, AND volatility.

Edge source: Defined risk (long options), leverage (limited capital at risk), volatility edge if implied vol is mispriced.
Risk: Time decay (theta) erodes value daily. Three variables must align: direction, magnitude, and timing. Most retail options traders lose. Covered in FINN Month 4+.
Momentum / Event-Driven
Horizon
1–5 Days
Min Capital
$5,000+
Difficulty
Level 4
What it is: Trading around specific catalysts — earnings, FDA decisions, macro data releases. Enter before or immediately after the event; exit once the move resolves.

Edge source: Post-earnings drift (documented anomaly), volatility compression before events, gap-and-go setups.
Risk: Binary outcomes. Wrong-direction gap can be 10–20%+ against you overnight. Requires precise sizing — treat every event trade as if it could go to zero.
Scalping / HFT-Adjacent
Horizon
Seconds–Minutes
Min Capital
$25,000+
Difficulty
Level 5
What it is: Capturing tiny price moves (cents to fractions of a percent) across dozens or hundreds of trades per day. Depends on ultra-fast execution and minimal spread.

Edge source: Order flow reading, Level 2 tape reading, market microstructure. Increasingly dominated by algorithms.
Risk: Spread and commission costs can erase the entire edge. Emotionally exhausting. Not viable as a retail strategy against HFT competition. Informational only in FINN.

FINN recommendation: Start with swing trading. It has the best ratio of screen time to learning value, allows proper backtesting, and is compatible with a day job and family. The systematic skills you build in swing trading transfer directly to every other style — the process is identical, only the timeframe changes.

The style determines the tools, not the edge. A 0.55 win rate with 2:1 reward-to-risk is a valid edge in any timeframe. The question is not "which style is best" but "which style fits my life, capital, and the signal I have tested." Answer those three questions before choosing.
