Multi-Timeframe Analysis

Top-down framework: Daily → 4H → 1H → 15min — context before entry, every time
Tier 2 · Technical Analysis

FINN framing rule: Every timeframe answers a different question. The daily tells you what the market is doing. The 4H tells you where structure sits. The 1H tells you when a pattern is forming. The 15min tells you exactly where to enter. Never use a lower timeframe to justify a trade that the higher timeframe doesn't support.

The Top-Down Cascade
Timeframe 1
Daily Chart
Trend + Context
Purpose
Define trend direction and major levels
  • Trend: above or below 20/50 EMA?
  • Higher highs + higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs + lower lows (downtrend)?
  • Key support and resistance zones
  • Any major gaps on the daily?
  • RSI: overbought, oversold, or neutral?
Gating question: What is the primary trend direction? All trades must be in this direction.
→
Timeframe 2
4-Hour Chart
Structure + Swings
Purpose
Locate intermediate structure and swing pivots
  • Is 4H trend aligned with daily trend?
  • Swing highs and lows on 4H
  • Key supply/demand zones between daily levels
  • Is price near a 4H S/R zone right now?
  • Volume confirmation of recent 4H moves
Gating question: Is there intermediate structure supporting the daily trend? Where is the next meaningful level?
→
Timeframe 3
1-Hour Chart
Pattern + Timing
Purpose
Identify the setup pattern and timing window
  • Is there a recognizable chart pattern forming?
  • Where is VWAP relative to price?
  • EMA 9/21 crossover or curl in trend direction?
  • Consolidation near a 4H level = setup signal
  • RSI returning from extreme = momentum reset
Gating question: Is a valid setup pattern present and completing? Am I early or at the right time?
→
Timeframe 4
15-Minute Chart
Entry + Stop
Purpose
Execute with precision — entry and stop placement
  • Trigger candle confirms direction (close, not wick)
  • Entry price: break + retest of level, or trigger close
  • Stop: below last 15min swing low (long) or above swing high (short)
  • Target: nearest 1H or 4H resistance/support
  • R:R ≥ 2:1 before entering
Gating question: Where exactly is my entry, stop, and target? Is the R:R worth executing?
◆
FINN MTF Rule: Trade the daily trend direction. Time the entry on the 15-minute chart. Every other timeframe is context — it narrows the playing field so the 15-minute trigger has the highest probability setup possible. If the daily says uptrend, you are looking for long setups on the 15min. Period.
Alignment Rules + Conflict Handling
When Timeframes Align (High-Probability)
1
Daily uptrend + 4H uptrend + 1H setup forming + 15min trigger = highest probability long setup. All four pointing the same direction is the rarest and best condition.
2
Three-timeframe alignment (daily + 4H + 1H) with 15min pending trigger = valid setup. Wait for the 15min trigger — do not force entry.
3
Daily and 4H aligned but 1H in pullback = wait. The pullback on 1H toward a 4H level is the setup building — not a signal to enter yet.
4
Daily at key level + 4H at same level + volume confirmation = structural trade with strong confluence. Size up appropriately.

FINN MTF Workflow — Every Trade, In Order
01
Daily
Read the trend
Uptrend or downtrend? Where are the major levels? What is RSI doing?
Gate: No clear trend = no trade today
02
4H
Find the structure
Swing pivots, S/R zones, volume profile. Is price near a meaningful 4H level?
Gate: No 4H level nearby = no trigger zone
03
1H
Wait for the setup
Pattern forming? VWAP position? EMA behavior? Is price consolidating at 4H level?
Gate: No pattern = wait, not enter
04
15min
Execute the trigger
Confirm trigger candle close. Set entry, stop, target. Verify R:R ≥ 2:1. Enter.
Gate: R:R < 2:1 = skip this setup
05
Manage
Monitor on 1H
Manage the trade on 1H. Trail stop to 4H swings. Exit at 4H target, not 15min noise.
Gate: Don't manage on 15min — 1H only

"Most traders pick a timeframe and live there. The edge is in the cascade — you use higher timeframes to eliminate low-probability trades so that by the time you reach the 15-minute trigger, you're not gambling on a candle, you're confirming the last piece of a structure that three timeframes already agreed on. That's not analysis. That's a process."
