Market Traps

Bull traps, bear traps, stop hunts, and false breakouts — identify them before they cost you
Tier 2 · Price Action

FINN framing rule: A trap is not bad luck. It is a predictable pattern that exploits where retail traders cluster their entries and stops. The edge is in knowing where those clusters form and what price action looks like before the reversal confirms. Volume and candlestick behavior at the level are the data. Read them, don't guess them.

The Four Traps
Bull Trap
False Breakout Above Resistance
Price closes above a key resistance level, draws in buyers, then reverses sharply back below — trapping longs at the high.
Diagram
  ┌─ Resistance ──────
  │   ↑ BREAK (trap)
──┘   ↓ reversal
      ↓
      ↓ trapped longs
      ↓ stopped out
Identify
  • Breakout candle has low or declining volume
  • Close is only slightly above resistance
  • Long upper wick on breakout candle
  • No expansion in volatility (ATR flat)
  • Reversal candle closes back inside range
Protect
  • Require volume confirmation ≥ 1.5× avg on breakout
  • Wait for candle close above resistance, not just wick
  • Look for retest of resistance-turned-support before entry
  • Stop below the reclaimed level, not the breakout candle low
Bear Trap
False Breakdown Below Support
Price closes below a key support level, draws in short sellers, then reverses sharply back above — trapping shorts at the low.
Diagram
  trapped shorts
  ↑ stopped out
  ↑ reversal
  ↓ BREAK (trap)
──┐
  └─ Support ─────────
Identify
  • Breakdown on below-average volume
  • Long lower wick on breakdown candle
  • Close only marginally below support
  • No follow-through selling the next candle
  • Reclaims support within 1–2 candles
Protect
  • Require expansion in sell volume before shorting breakdown
  • Wait for a candle close below support on meaningful spread
  • Retest of support-turned-resistance as short entry
  • Stop above reclaimed level; size for the R, not the fear
Stop Hunt
Deliberate Spike to Trigger Clustered Stops
Large players engineer a brief price spike beyond an obvious level where retail stops are clustered, trigger those stops, accumulate/distribute, then reverse into the real move.
Diagram
  ── obvious high ──
  ↑ spike (wick)
  ↑ stops triggered
  ↓ fast reversal
  ↓ real move begins
  ───────────────────
Identify
  • Extremely long wick with small body (pin bar)
  • Spike to/through obvious swing high or low
  • High volume on the spike candle
  • Fast rejection — reversal within same or next candle
  • Occurs most at round numbers, prior day highs/lows
Protect
  • Do not place stops at obvious swing points — offset beyond the cluster
  • Use ATR-based stop placement, not round numbers
  • Wait for rejection candle to close before entering the real move
  • The spike itself can be a signal: trade the reversal, not the spike
False Breakout
Breakout That Fails to Sustain
A breakout above or below a level that initially appears valid but fails to attract follow-through momentum, reverses back through the level, and continues in the opposite direction.
Diagram
  ── Level ──────────
  ↑ break
  ↑ stall (no follow)
  ↓ returns to level
  ↓ continues lower
  ───────────────────
Identify
  • Breakout volume fades within 2–3 candles
  • Price stalls without reaching next resistance/support
  • Multiple attempts to hold level all fail
  • RSI divergence at breakout candle
  • VWAP rejection on intraday frames
Protect
  • Confirm breakout with expanding volume AND increasing spread
  • Check higher-timeframe alignment before entering breakout
  • Use a timer: if no follow-through in N candles, reassess
  • When a false breakout confirms, the opposite trade is the edge

Universal Protection Rules
01
Volume is the lie detector
Legitimate breakouts expand volume. Traps either use thin volume or spike briefly then collapse. No volume confirmation = no entry.
02
Wait for the close
Wicks are noise. The candle close above or below a level is the data point. Entering on a wick that hasn't closed is guessing, not trading.
03
Offset your stops
Stops at obvious swing points are inventory for stop hunts. Use ATR × 1.5 beyond the level. Make your stop structurally logical, not round-number obvious.
04
The failed trap is a trade
When a bull trap confirms (price reclaims resistance-turned-support), that's a long setup. When a bear trap confirms, that's a short. Traps signal trapped traders who must cover.
