#### Block 2 — Full Course Synthesis (8:15–8:55 PM) | 40 min

No new content. A structured synthesis conversation.

**Part 1 — From Month 1 to Month 6 (15 min):**

Instructor leads: "Walk me back through the course.
Month 1: what did you think a trading strategy was?
Month 2: what changed when you built your first backtest?
Month 3: what surprised you in the first live week?

The goal is not nostalgia — it is connecting the intellectual arc. Students should articulate how each month built on the last. Instructor notes: any student who can't connect Month 5 to Month 2 — their presentation will reflect that gap. Flag it now.

**Part 2 — The Presentation Structure (25 min)**

"The Milestone 4 presentation is 20 minutes. Structure it this way:

**Opening (2 min):** State the hypothesis. Not the strategy description — the hypothesis. 'I tested whether [specific edge] exists in [specific market] using [specific approach]. Here is what I found.'

**Design (3 min):** What you built — architecture, signals, risk framework. Show the feature importance chart. Show the signal score formula. Be precise. One slide per major component.

**Evidence (7 min):** What the data showed. Lead with the equity curve and benchmark comparison. Then: attribution, rolling Sharpe, regime map. State the key numbers. Reference every chart.

**AI Integration (3 min):** What the ML and sentiment components actually contributed vs. the rules-based baseline. Use the quartile chart and the IC comparison. Be honest — if they added nothing, say so and explain why.

**Limitations and Learning (3 min):** The three most important limitations. The most important unexpected finding. What changed in your thinking.

**Forward Plan (2 min):** Three specific improvements with evidence basis. What you would test first with more time.

**Q&A (10 min):** Expect hard questions on methodology. Have the evidence ready. 'The data shows ___' beats 'I think ___' every time."

After the structure walkthrough: each student states their opening sentence — the hypothesis statement, as they plan to deliver it. Instructor gives immediate feedback on specificity and precision.

#### Milestone 4 Presentation Structure (30 min per student)

**Presentation (20 min):**
Six sections as designed in Monday's session. Timed. Hard stop at 20 minutes.

**Q&A (10 min):**
Instructor leads. Class may ask after instructor questions.

**Q&A Question Bank — Milestone 4:**

*On hypothesis and evidence:*
- "Your hypothesis was [restate]. In one sentence — did the evidence support it?"
- "What would it take to convince you this edge is real and repeatable?"
- "If you ran this strategy for 12 more months, what outcome would most surprise you?"

*On technical execution:*
- "Walk me through your XGBoost model. What is it actually predicting? How confident are you in the OOS IC result?"
- "Show me one trade where the ML signal and the rules signal disagreed. Which was right? What does that tell you?"
- "Your backtest used a 5-fold TimeSeriesSplit. How many total data points were in each fold? Was each fold long enough to be statistically meaningful?"

*On AI integration:*
- "The sentiment component had weight W. Based on your results, was that weight justified? What IC evidence do you have?"
- "If you removed the sentiment veto entirely — what would have changed? How many trades would have been different?"

*On limitations and self-assessment:*
- "What is the single most important thing your backtest assumed that live trading revealed was wrong?"
- "You described [limitation]. How confident are you that you've identified the real cause rather than a surface symptom?"
- "Your rule adherence was X%. For the trades where you deviated — what was the average P&L compared to rule-following trades?"

*On forward plan:*
- "You said you would [improvement 1]. What specific data would tell you after 30 trades whether that improvement worked?"
- "If you had to launch this strategy at a prop desk tomorrow — what is the first thing you would change about the infrastructure?"

## INSTRUCTOR NOTES FOR WEEK 24

**The hardest coaching moment this week is the student who underperformed:** A student whose Sharpe was 0.3 and who underperformed SPY significantly will struggle with the evidence section. The coaching is: underperformance analyzed honestly is more impressive than outperformance analyzed vaguely. A student who can say "my strategy underperformed by 4% relative to SPY, and the attribution shows this was primarily due to the strategy's underexposure to the tech momentum trend that drove SPY's outperformance in Month 6, combined with a 30% false signal rate from the sentiment veto during earnings season" has demonstrated more analytical capability than one who says "my strategy returned 8% and beat SPY." Hold this standard explicitly.

**Rehearsal timing issues are always the same:** Students always spend too long on Design and not enough on Limitations and Forward Plan. The Design section is comfortable — they built it. The Limitations section requires intellectual vulnerability. If a student is running long in rehearsal, the cut almost always comes from Design, not Evidence. Be direct: "You spent 5 minutes on architecture. You need 3. The presentation earns points on self-assessment and Q&A. That's where the time goes."

**Q&A is where the real assessment happens:** A polished 20-minute presentation can hide surface-level understanding. The Q&A cannot. The question bank is designed to surface: (1) whether the student knows their own data, (2) whether they understand the statistical limitations of their results, (3) whether they have real ideas for improvement vs. generic answers. Prepare personalized questions for each student based on their specific strategy and the gaps you observed in the report.

**The course close is a deliberate ritual:** Don't rush through it. The group retrospective questions are carefully chosen to produce genuine reflection — what students thought trading was vs. what it turned out to be. Some students will have strong emotional responses. That is appropriate. Six months of work, every week, with a real paper portfolio, deserves a real close.

**Post-course follow-up:** Deliver written Milestone 4 feedback within 5 days. Include the skill progression comparison (Month 1 vs. Month 3 vs. Month 6) — this is often the most meaningful feedback students receive. Most of them significantly underestimate how much they have learned.
