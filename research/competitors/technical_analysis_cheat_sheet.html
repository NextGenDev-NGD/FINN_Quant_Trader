How to Read a Candle

Large Body Strong conviction. Buyers (green) or sellers (red) dominated the session decisively. High follow-through probability.
Small Body Indecision or balance. Neither side dominated. Watch for a decisive break in either direction.
Long Upper Wick Price rallied and was rejected. Bears pushed back and won the session's high. Bearish signal — especially at resistance.
Long Lower Wick Price sold off and was bought back. Bulls rejected the lows. Bullish signal — especially at support.
No Wicks Marubozu candle. Price opened and closed at extremes — strongest possible momentum candle.
Gaps Open ≠ prior close. Strong overnight demand (gap up) or supply (gap down). Often unfilled on strong trends.
Body size matters most. A candle's body tells you who won; the wicks tell you who tried and failed.

Key Candlestick Patterns

Bullish Engulfing

▲ BULLISH REVERSAL

Large green candle fully engulfs prior red candle's body. Strongest at bottom of downtrend. Body must overlap 100%.

At support High volume 2-candle

Bearish Engulfing

▼ BEARISH REVERSAL

Large red candle fully engulfs prior green candle's body. Strongest at top of uptrend or at resistance levels.

At resistance High volume 2-candle

Hammer

▲ BULLISH REVERSAL

Small body at top, long lower wick ≥ 2× body length. Price was rejected at lows. Must occur after a downtrend.

After downtrend Wick ≥ 2× body

Shooting Star

▼ BEARISH REVERSAL

Small body at bottom, long upper wick ≥ 2× body. Price rallied and was fully rejected. Must occur after an uptrend.

After uptrend Wick ≥ 2× body

Doji

◆ INDECISION

Open and close nearly equal. Complete balance of buyers and sellers. Signals potential reversal when at an extreme. Dragonfly / Gravestone variants are directional.

Context-dependent Confirm next candle

Pin Bar

▲ STRONG REVERSAL

Tiny body with an extreme wick (the "nose"). The wick punctures a key level and is rejected hard. Wick ≥ 3× body for high quality. Best at S/R, POC, or VWAP.

At key level Wick ≥ 3× body

Inside Bar

◆ CONTINUATION / BREAKOUT

Second candle (IB) is completely within the first candle's (mother bar) high-low range. A pause before the next move. Trade the breakout of the mother bar.

Break high = bull Break low = bear

Morning Star

▲ BULLISH REVERSAL (3-candle)

Bearish → small indecision → strong bullish. Third candle must close above 50% of first candle's body. Evening Star is the inverse bearish version.

3-candle pattern C3 closes above 50%

Market Structure — BOS & CHoCH

Uptrend Structure

Series of Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL). As long as the most recent HL holds, the trend is intact. Trade with the trend — not against it.

Lower High (LH) — First Warning

Price fails to make a new high. The uptrend is weakening. Not yet a reversal — but stop adding to longs and tighten stops on existing positions.

Break of Structure (BOS)

Price breaks below the most recent Higher Low. The trend's foundation is cracked. For a downtrend BOS, price breaks above the most recent Lower High.

Change of Character (CHoCH)

A new Lower Low forms after the BOS. The uptrend is officially over — a confirmed downtrend has begun. This is the full reversal signal.

BOS ≠ CHoCH. BOS is the warning — the first structural crack. CHoCH is the confirmation — the new trend is now printing its first Lower Low or Higher High.

Volume Tools — Profile & VWAP

VOLUME PROFILE

Point of Control (POC)

The single price level with the highest traded volume. Price treats POC as a magnet — it will frequently return to it. When price is above POC it acts as support; below POC it acts as resistance.

Naked POC: A prior session's POC that price has not returned to since. Strong magnet — will usually get revisited.

Value Area (VAH / VAL)

The price range containing 70% of a session's volume. VAH and VAL are natural boundaries. Price returning inside the value area from outside tends to travel to the POC. Price breaking and holding outside VA tends to continue in that direction.

VAH = resistance VAL = support 70% rule

HVN vs LVN

High Volume Nodes (HVN): Strong S/R. Price slows and consolidates here — lots of orders to work through.

Low Volume Nodes (LVN): Little resistance. Price moves fast through these areas. Between an HVN and LVN, expect acceleration once the HVN breaks.

VWAP — Volume Weighted Average Price

The "fair price" for the day, weighted by volume. Institutions use VWAP as an execution benchmark — they try to buy below it and sell above it.

Standard deviation bands (±1σ, ±2σ) define the expected range. Price at VWAP+2σ is extended; at VWAP it is at fair value.

Anchored VWAP: VWAP anchored from a specific event (earnings, major low). Tracks institutions who positioned at that event.

Resets daily Mean reversion at extremes Trend anchor intraday

Supply & Demand Zones

Supply Zone

A price area where price previously consolidated, then dropped aggressively. Institutions were selling. When price returns here, those same institutions are likely to sell again — creating resistance.

Drop-Base-Drop: Price drops → consolidates → drops again. Zone forms at the top of that base.

Rally-Base-Drop: Price rallies → consolidates → reverses sharply lower. Zone forms at the base.

Demand Zone

A price area where price consolidated and then rallied aggressively. Institutional buyers were accumulating. When price returns, they buy again — creating support.

Rally-Base-Rally: Price rallies → consolidates → rallies again. Zone at base.

Drop-Base-Rally: Price drops → consolidates → reverses sharply higher.

Zone Quality Criteria

  • Fresh zone: Never been retested — strongest version. A zone loses strength each time price touches it (orders get filled).
  • Strong departure: The bigger the move away from the zone, the more unfilled orders remain at that level.
  • Time: Older zones weaken as institutional interests change. Zones from major market events (earnings, macro) are the most durable.
  • Tight consolidation: A narrow, well-defined base indicates precision institutional positioning — cleaner entry levels.
Supply/Demand zones answer why price reacts, not just where. They are the institutional fingerprint on the chart.

ATR & Risk Management

What ATR Measures

Average True Range — the average daily volatility over the last N periods (standard: 14). "True Range" = max of: (High−Low), (High−Prior Close), (Low−Prior Close). ATR always adapts to current market conditions.

ATR(14) = Average of True Range over last 14 bars
True Range = max( H−L, |H−Prev C|, |L−Prev C| )

ATR Stop Loss Sizing

Set stops at a multiple of ATR below entry — not at arbitrary levels. This adapts your stop to current volatility automatically.

Stop = Entry Price − (ATR × multiplier)

Conservative : 1.0× ATR
Standard : 1.5× ATR
Aggressive : 2.0× ATR

Example: Stock at $50.00, ATR = $1.20, 1.5× multiplier → Stop at $50.00 − $1.80 = $48.20

Position Sizing with ATR

Determine how many shares to buy so that if stopped out, you lose exactly your risk budget. Keeps risk consistent regardless of stock volatility.

Risk per trade = Account × Risk %
Stop distance = ATR × multiplier
Shares = Risk per trade ÷ Stop distance

Example: $10,000 account, 1% risk = $100. ATR = $1.20, stop = 1.5× = $1.80. Shares = $100 ÷ $1.80 = 55 shares

ATR as Volatility Filter

Use ATR to screen for trading opportunities that match your strategy's requirements:

  • Momentum strategies: ATR expanding → volatility increasing → stronger moves expected
  • Mean reversion: ATR contracting → range-bound → fade moves at extremes
  • Minimum ATR threshold: Avoid stocks with ATR < $0.50 — too little movement for meaningful risk/reward

Risk:Reward & ATR Together

Target = Entry + (Stop Distance × R:R ratio)

Stop dist = 1.5× ATR = $1.80
2:1 target = Entry + $3.60
3:1 target = Entry + $5.40
Minimum 2:1 R:R 3:1 preferred Never < 1.5:1

Quick Reference — Rules of Thumb

Price Action Rules

  • Engulfing body must fully cover prior body
  • Hammer/Pin wick ≥ 2–3× body length
  • Always confirm with next candle close
  • Pattern + key level = high-probability setup
  • Volume confirmation = required

Volume Rules

  • Value Area = 70% of session volume
  • POC = most traded price = magnet
  • Breakout on low volume = likely fake
  • Naked POC always gets revisited
  • HVN = slowdown; LVN = fast move

Structure Rules

  • Trade WITH the structure, not against it
  • BOS = first warning, not entry signal
  • CHoCH = trend officially changed
  • LH before BOS = early warning sign
  • Fresh supply/demand = strongest setups

Risk Rules

  • Never risk > 1–2% of account per trade
  • Stop = 1.5× ATR from entry
  • Minimum 2:1 risk-to-reward
  • Size position: Risk $ ÷ Stop distance
  • ATR expanding = bigger stops needed

The core framework: Read price action to know what is happening now. Use volume to confirm who is in control. Use market structure to know which direction to trade. Use supply/demand zones to find where to enter. Use ATR to know how much to risk. Master these five — ignore the rest.
